Every developer is blocked by some version of these barriers when trying to contribute to open source.

"I'm not experienced enough."

Whether you're stuck looking for the perfect "beginner issue" or terrified of making a mistake in front of everyone, you're not alone. You've fallen prey to some of the most common open source myths. I can help unblock your fears and unlock your potential.

"There's just not enough time."

If you've found yourself thinking, "I would love to contribute, but I just don't have the time," you're not alone. Time is constantly in short supply. The day always seems to end before you can write that issue response or start researching that feature idea. Unfortunately, I don't have a way to give you more hours in the day. Instead, we'll look at how successful contributors find the time and how you can do it too.

Sounds great. What topics are covered exactly?

  • Go beyond simple "how to" tasks to understand WHY contributors are successful.
  • Find your next contribution opportunity with "COIL" - a four-step repeatable framework.
  • Use concepts such as "floss one tooth" and "bat 300" to get over mental barriers and inertia to get started and keep going.
  • Shift from a mindset of "beginner issue" to "beginning action".
  • Move issues and bug reports along by increasing the quality of reproduction instructons.
  • Write A+ documentation, even for code you didn't write.
  • Maximize your chance in getting your pull request merged..
  • Apply non-violent communication principles and navigate conversations that get tense.
  • See real life examples on how other people fit open source time into their jobs.
  • Sustain your contributions beyond one commit to achieve lasting impact.

Frequently Asked Questions

I saw your videos, but I still feel like I'm not ready. Where do I start?

Start gradually and then slowly increase complexity. A core concept in the book comes from "floss one tooth". In short, it can be daunting to make a large change. Flossing ALL your teeth feels daunting. But what about just one? Show up and do it every day. One day you might find yourself flossing all of them. In the same spirit, my book helps you find the smallest action possible and helps you gradually level up.

There are a lot of videos and guides online which didn't help me. They teach you git and expect you to contribute to Angular next. How will your book be different?

There's a big difference between knowing the mechanics of making a pull request and making a useful pull request. In my book, I'm focusing on concrete and actionable skills. I've included exercises and real-world examples. All of these combined will give you the confidence and skills needed to make an impactful contribution.

I love your writing and subscribe to your blog already. Is this content recycling what you've already written, or is it totally new?

"How to Open Source" book is all new content. While writing this book, it was important that I don't just list off a series of tips and tricks. Through my time teaching at the University of Texas, I know the importance of stories for retention. I loaded my chapters with examples that are easy to follow and will help you recall what you need when you need it.

Will this book teach me how to get a thousand stars on my GitHub repo?

Before jumping into the driver's seat as a maintainer, I recommend you practice being a contributor. This book's core focus is on developing those contribution skills and building a sustainable practice. If you're primarily interested in content on maintenance, you'll still need to build skills in responding to issues, writing documentation, and interacting on PRs.

I simply don't have deep enough experience to contribute yet. How will I know I'm ready for this book?

I believe that we're all beginners in life. I believe in a growth mindset and that everyone is filled with experiences that bring a unique perspective. Your unique perspective is why you're valuable to open source. You can see things others can't. If you can write code without following a tutorial, then you're primed to get the most out of this book. No prior open source experience required.

I tried to contribute to a repo but couldn't even get a response from the maintainer. Will this book help?

Maintainers are in high demand. Getting their attention can be tricky but not impossible. I walk through my experiences with this all-too-common scenario and share specific, respectful strategies to move the conversation closer to getting your PR merged. You'll also learn about the hidden dynamics of maintaining an open source repo. When you understand what maintainers want, you can maximize your chances of getting your PR merged.

Why are you charging for a book on open source instead of giving it away for free?

The book has a creative commons license and no DRM. I wish I could give everything away for free: the book, the illustrations, my videos, everything. I chose to make CodeTriage free and open source for all because I believe the community is a better place when everyone has access. I learned from my time publishing my free Rails course that giving away content can have an enormous impact, but content is harder to sustain than code. I've labored over this book for years and am proud of the result. I charge because I think it's worth the value.
