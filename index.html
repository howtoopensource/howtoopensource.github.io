Everything you need to get started

Start contributing, build actionable skills, and create sustainable habits.

What to start contributing but feel like you're stuck? Think you don't know know enough to help? Worried about being exposed as some programmer fraud? Tried to contribute before but got nowhere?

The good news is that you're not alone. Many developers face these barriers. Together we'll face the fear and explore practical methods to overcome the most common contribution problems

Confused about what to do next? This book lays out clear and actionable steps that anyone can take to start contributing to any open source project. You'll find contribution stories and real-world examples of open source contributions ranging from reproducing bugs and responding to issues, to updating documentation (even for code you didn't write), and getting your first commit merged in a pull request.

Did you already get a commit merged but then lost steam? Most contributors leave a project after one commit. Even successful contributors need help creating a contribution practice and building habits that work with their lifestyle. Beyond contributing superficial changes, developers who follow this guide will walk away ready to make an impact and achieve their contribution goals.

See the Table of contents for more details.

Strong foundations

Go beyond simple "how to" tasks to understand WHY contributors are successful.

Are you tired of tutorials that tell you what to do, but not why? "How to Open Source" breaks down complicated concepts into managable frameworks.

For example, every open source contribution consists of four parts:

  • Context building
  • Opportunity finding
  • Implementation
  • Looping

Together these actions make up the COIL acronym. In this book you'll learn how to use COIL and other frameworks to break down complicated tasks into managable pieces. When you're done with this book you'll be able to make your contribution ideas into reality.

Takeaways TOGO

Optimized for learning and reference.

Do you love reading real life stories and examples, but find it remember the important details later when you need them? "How to Open Source" comes with reference-ready cheatsheets to help you jog your memory and make sure you don't forget any crucial steps.

Whether you're a brand new to open source or you're a seasoned contributor, there's something for everone to help maximize your impact.

Cheatsheets included for sections on:

  • Becoming familiar with an open source project and identifying contribution opportunities.
  • Responding to issues and bug reports on any open source repository.
  • Writing A+ documentation, even for code you didn't write.
  • Everything you need to know about the pull request process to maximize mergability.

The Author

Richard Schneeman, O'Reilly Author & creator of CodeTriage

"I wrote the book I wish someone had given me 10 years ago when I got started contributing"

Richard has spent the last half-decade researching how to bridge the gap between projects that want contributors and people that want to contribute. He has conducted interviews, paired with developers, and made hundreds of personal contributions. He is most known for helping sixty-thousand developers contribute to open source through his platform, CodeTriage.

He is a maintainer of libraries with over 1.9+ billion downloads. He currently helps maintain the Puma webserver, a high performance threaded webserver in Ruby. He contributes to high profile projects including the Ruby language and created several successful open source projects such as derailed benchmarks with over 2.7 thousand stars on github. In addition to Ruby, Richard enjoys writing Rust and "any programming language that will get the job done".

Richard lives in Austin, Texas with his partner, two kids, and two dogs.
